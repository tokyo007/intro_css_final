Examples of Stocks based on their market capitalization size

Investing by market capitalization size is one method for investing in the stock market. There are other methods based on valuation ratios, technical indicators, geography as well as industry classification.

To invest by market capitalization, it is important to understand the meaning of market capitalization. Market capitalization is defined as a company's current stock price multiplied by the number of shares outstanding.

Typically, market capitalization is divided into several groups; Large Cap, Mid Cap, Small Cap and Micro Cap. This site lists a sample of stocks found in the large cap, mid cap and small cap groups. Micro caps are ignored as they are typically the riskiest stocks on the stock market.

Investing using market capitalization is a method that is used by many investors. It is a method that is easy to understand and is a good starting point for new investors. It is also a method that is used by many professional investors.
